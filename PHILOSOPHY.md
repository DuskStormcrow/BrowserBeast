# BrowserBeast Philosophy

BrowserBeast exists to solve one problem well: turning what you see in a browser
into a clean, structured artifact you can use later.

That's it.

## Capture, don't transform

BrowserBeast captures what's there. It does not rewrite, summarize, classify,
or enrich content. The artifact is yours — what you captured, when you captured
it, from where.

## User-triggered, always

BrowserBeast runs when you click. It never runs in the background, never
monitors your tabs, never captures anything automatically. The user is in
control of every capture.

## Local-first

Nothing BrowserBeast captures leaves your machine as part of its normal
workflow. There is no sync, no cloud, no server. The Markdown file is yours.

## Markdown-first

Markdown is the artifact. Plain text with structure — human-readable,
machine-readable, long-lived. The Evidence Envelope is embedded inside the
Markdown, not stored separately.

## Evidence Envelope

Structured metadata matters. The Evidence Envelope exists so captures can be
identified, searched, indexed, and traced later — even outside of BrowserBeast.
It records what was captured, where it came from, when, and under what conditions.

It does not redact. It does not certify. It records.

## Privacy-advisory, not privacy-enforcement

BrowserBeast warns when it detects private or logged-in indicators. It cannot
know what is actually sensitive on every page. The warning is advisory — a
prompt to review before sharing, not a guarantee of safety.

Users are responsible for reviewing captures before sharing them.

## Simplicity is a feature

The popup has only a few core actions. That is intentional. BrowserBeast should be
learnable in under a minute and fast enough that using it does not interrupt
your flow.

Features that add complexity without improving capture quality, reliability,
or privacy safety do not belong in BrowserBeast. PNG screenshots, AI summaries,
automatic sync, and cloud storage are not on the roadmap — not because they
are bad ideas, but because BrowserBeast is not that tool.

## Sustainability

No dependencies. No build step. No framework. Vanilla JavaScript, Chrome
Manifest V3, plain CSS. This keeps BrowserBeast maintainable by one person,
easy to audit, and resistant to dependency rot.

A tool that still works in five years is worth more than one that is impressive
today and broken tomorrow.
